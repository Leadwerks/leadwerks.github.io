Texturing Solids

You can apply textures to solids, or to the separate faces of a solid. You can also adjust the texture mapping parameters to change the way a texture lies on a solid.

Texturing Solids

To apply a texture to a selected solid, choose the texture you want from the texture category and texture lists, located in the side panel.

Press the apply texture toolbar button to paint the selection with the current texture.

Texturing Solid Faces

To apply a texture to a single face of a solid, first press the select subobject toolbar button to switch to subobject mode. Select the face you wish to texture.

Press the apply texture toolbar button to paint the selected face with the current texture.

If you want to see the texture on the selected face more easily, you can press the hide mask toolbar button. This will hide the red selection mask.

Texture Mapping Properties

The texture mapping properties control the way a texture lies on a solid face. To view the texture mapping properties, press the select subobject toolbar button to switch to subobject mode. Select a solid face. Its texture mapping properties will appear in the side panel.

Shift
The shift property will offset a texture.

Scale
The scale property will make a texture smaller or larger.

Rotation
The rotation property controls a rotation angle for the texture.

You can skew the texture by unchecking the "One Angle" checkbox and setting two different rotation angles.

Justify
The justify buttons will align the texture to an edge, center it, or stretch it over a face. This is much easier than trying to adjust the texture mapping properties by hand. If you uncheck the "One Face" checkbox, the justify operations will be performed on each face as though it was the only one selected.

Texture Lock

The texture lock tool will prevent a texture from moving when you move a solid. Press the texture lock toolbar button. You can now move solids, and their texture mapping will stay locked in place.
